Culture-centered User Experience

An international communication research project

This is a semester research project for my International Communication class in the UNC Journalism School. We could choose any topic that related to the class as well as which format would be best for conveying our findings. I was first introduced to the conundrum of designing for international audiences during my internship at Microsoft. Windows is used by 85% of computer users as of July 2013. Obviously they face this issue and may or may not be succeeding in tackling it. I was surprised to learn how difficult it is for a designer to find conceptual metaphors that make sense to people from another culture.

Click here to see the website for my project

I chose to present my project through a website because it would be more accessible to the general public than the suggested 16-page paper. I hope people will spend more time learning and thinking about this issue. I also wanted to include some cool interactive data visualizations for the site, including a map of the growth of Internet access from 1990 to 2012 and a dynamic bar graph tool for comparing cultures in different countries. See below for screenshots or visit the site. Note: This site is not accessible on mobile platforms due to complexity of the content and limited time for the school project.

Over the next couple of weeks I will wrap up my project with case studies comparing versions of the McDonalds and Nokia homepages that were designed for different cultures. I look forward to analyzing their research and seeing which things they changed and which elements remained the same on the various sites. I will update this page once it is over with a more in-depth report of my process throughout the project. Stay tuned!
